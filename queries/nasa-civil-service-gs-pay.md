# NASA Civil Service — GS Pay, Benefits, and Comparison

> Curiosity query — Rick is NOT interested in civil service employment. This page exists for reference and to document why the GS system doesn't match his market value.

## Entry Level for Rick's Credentials

- M.S. in Modeling & Simulation + 7+ years specialized NASA engineering experience
- Likely entry: **GS-13 (Step 5-10)** or **GS-14 (Step 1-3)**
- NASA has Superior Qualifications authority to set pay above Step 1
- NASA engineering positions at MSFC typically run GS-13 through GS-15

## GS Pay Cap

- Tied to Executive Schedule Level IV (EX-IV)
- 2025 cap: **$183,500**
- 2026 projected cap: **~$191,800**
- Even GS-15 Step 10 cannot exceed this cap, regardless of locality calculation

## FERS Pension (The Big Advantage)

Three-tier retirement system:
1. FERS Basic Benefit (defined benefit pension)
2. TSP (defined contribution, like 401k)
3. Social Security

### Contribution
- 4.4% of base pay (FERS-FRAE, for new hires after 2013)

### Pension Formula
- High-3 Average Salary x Years of Service x Multiplier
- Multiplier: 1% per year (1.1% if retiring at 62+ with 20+ years)

### Example for Rick
- 20 years at GS-14 Step 10 (~$185K high-3 average)
- 20 years x 1.0% = 20%
- Annual pension: $185,000 x 0.20 = **$37,000/year** (lifetime, with COLAs)
- If retiring at 62+ with 20+ years: $185,000 x 0.22 = **$40,700/year**

### Minimum Retirement Age
- MRA is 57 (born after 1969)
- Retire at MRA with 30 years, age 60 with 20 years, or age 62 with 5 years

## TSP Matching (Better Than Amentum)

| Component | Agency Contribution |
|---|---|
| Automatic contribution | 1% of base pay (free, no contribution required) |
| Match on first 3% | 100% match (dollar-for-dollar) |
| Match on next 2% (3-5%) | 50% match |
| **Total maximum** | **5% of base pay** |

- At GS-14 Step 1 ($139,357): contributing 5% ($6,968) gets total TSP = $12,546/year
- Compare Amentum: ~$5,100/year (50% of 6%)
- 2025 contribution limit: $23,500/year; catch-up $7,500 (age 50+)

## FEHB Health Insurance

- ~75% premium paid by government (vs. varies as contractor)
- 75+ plan choices (BCBS Standard, BCBS Basic, GEHA, HMOs, HDHPs)
- No pre-existing condition exclusions, guaranteed issue
- **Continues into retirement** if 5+ years of coverage — major advantage
- Typical family costs: BCBS Standard ~$700/mo employee share, BCBS Basic ~$420/mo

## NASA Civil Service vs. Contractor Comparison

| Factor | Civil Service (NASA) | Contractor (Amentum) |
|---|---|---|
| Job security across contract transitions | Immune — employed by NASA regardless | Vulnerable — contract transitions every 5-10 years |
| Pension | FERS defined benefit (~$37K+/yr at retirement) | None — 401k only |
| 401k/TSP match | 5% + 1% automatic (~$7K at GS-14) | ~3% (~$5,100 at $170K) |
| Health insurance | FEHB, 75% gov't paid, continues in retirement | Varies, may change with contract |
| Job stability | Very high — civil service protections | At-will, vulnerable to contract loss |
| Leave | 13-26 days annual + 13 sick + 11 holidays | Typically less generous |
| Pay ceiling | ~$183K-$192K (EX-IV cap) | No statutory cap (contract-dependent) |
| Salary growth | Time-based step increases (1-3 years between) | Performance/contract-dependent |
| Pay flexibility | None — table-based | Can negotiate raises |

## Why It Doesn't Work for Rick

1. **Pay cut of $15K-$30K initially** — and his React+AI skills are worth $200K-$280K in private sector
2. **GS pay cap ~$192K** — he'd never match private sector React+AI ceiling
3. **Time-in-grade system** — rewards tenure, not skills. Rick's React+TypeScript+AI+UX intersection is not valued by the GS classification system.
4. **Limited salary negotiation** — can't negotiate above the table (except Superior Qualifications at entry)
5. **Slower advancement** — step increases are 1-3 years between; promotion to GS-15 is competitive and limited
6. **AGE — Rick is 49. The FERS pension requires long tenure to be meaningful:**
   - Starting at 49, he'd need to work until **69** (20 years) to get a 20% pension — $37K/year at GS-14 high-3
   - Retiring at 62 with 13 years of service = only **13% pension** (~$24K/year) — barely worth the pay cut
   - To get the 1.1% multiplier (age 62+ with 20+ years), he'd need to work until **69+**
   - FERS MRA is 57, but that requires 30 years of service for full pension — impossible starting at 49
   - The pension is designed for career federal employees who start in their 20s-30s. At 49, Rick simply doesn't have enough years left to accumulate meaningful benefit.
   - **The math: 20 years of ~$25K/yr pay cut = $500K in lost salary, for a $37K/yr pension starting at age 69.** Break-even is ~13.5 years into retirement (age 82+). That's a terrible trade.

## The One Scenario Where It Might Make Sense

If Rick were prioritizing stability and a pension over maximizing income — e.g., if family circumstances made job security paramount and the ESSCA contract situation became unstable. The FERS pension + FEHB-in-retirement + job security across contract transitions is a genuinely valuable combination for risk-averse career planning.

But given Rick's skill set, market position, and interest in React+AI/freelance work, the GS system is a ceiling he'd hit immediately.
