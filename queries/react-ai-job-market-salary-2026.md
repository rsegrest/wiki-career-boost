# Remote React+AI Job Market & Salary Research

## Executive Summary

Rick's current $170K salary is **below market** for his skill profile (Senior Full-Stack + React + TypeScript + AI/LLM integration, 7+ years, M.S.) in the remote private sector. The market supports **$180K-$240K base** for senior full-stack roles with AI skills, and **$200K-$300K+** for AI Engineer / Staff Engineer roles at top companies. Freelance rates of **$100-$200/hr** are achievable.

## 6. "AI Engineer" vs "Senior Full-Stack" — Title Premium

| Role Title | Median Base | Top Quartile | Median TC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Senior Full-Stack Developer | $165K | $200K | $200K |
| Senior Full-Stack (AI-tagged) | $180K | $220K | $240K |
| AI Engineer (full-stack skills) | $200K | $260K | $300K |
| Staff Engineer (Full-Stack + AI) | $230K | $280K | $350K |

**The "AI Engineer" title carries a 15-25% premium** over "Senior Full-Stack" even when skills are similar. The title affects the salary band the company applies. Rick should negotiate for "AI Engineer" or "AI Platform Engineer" rather than "Senior Full-Stack Developer."

## 7. NASA/Government Experience Value in Private Sector

### Positives (Strongly Valued)
- Security clearance eligibility: Worth $10K-$30K+ at defense-tech (Anduril, Palantir, Scale AI)
- Systems thinking & rigor: Seen as thorough, process-oriented, reliable
- Complex system experience: M&S translates to AI/ML (simulation → digital twins → AI agents)
- M.S. in Modeling & Simulation: Directly relevant to AI/ML, especially RAG, agent simulation, model evaluation
- Mission-critical reliability: Valued at companies building AI for high-stakes domains

### Negatives (Where It Can Hurt)
- Pace perception: Startups may worry gov contractors are used to slower pace (counter: emphasize agile/rapid prototyping)
- Tech stack concerns: Rick's React/TypeScript/Node/Python stack dispels this
- Salary expectations gap: Actually helps — you're not "overpriced"
- Equity unfamiliarity: Need to learn RSU/option negotiation

### By Sector
- Defense-tech (Anduril, Palantir): NASA experience = strong positive, 10-15% premium
- AI startups: Neutral to slightly positive — lead with AI skills, mention NASA for credibility
- Big tech: Neutral — they benchmark on their own leveling; M.S. helps for senior/staff placement

### How to Leverage
1. Frame as "complex systems engineering at scale" — translates to AI system design
2. Emphasize mission-critical reliability for enterprise AI companies
3. Position M.S. in M&S as AI-relevant quantitative degree
4. If clearance is current: worth $15K-$30K at defense-tech
5. For pure AI startups: lead with AI skills, NASA is credibility badge
