## ESSCA II Labor Category Descriptions

### ES-8 Engineer/Scientist (Rick's likely level)
- Description: Serves as a highly proficient engineer who can fulfill all functions
- Typical titles: Senior Supervisor, Technical Assistant, Chief Engineer, Chief Scientist
- Education: BS + ~10 years (M.S. = +2 years, so ~8 years with M.S.)

### ES-7 Engineer/Scientist (alternative classification)
- Description: Serves as a highly proficient engineer who can fulfill all functions
- Typical titles: Team Lead, Supervisor, Manager, Technical Assistant, Chief Engineer
- Education: BS + ~8 years (M.S. = +2 years, so ~6 years with M.S.)

### ES-6 Engineer/Scientist
- Description: Serves as a highly proficient engineer
- Typical titles: Senior Engineer, Senior Scientist, Lead Discipline Engineer
- Education: BS + ~6 years

### ES-5 Engineer/Scientist
- Description: Serves as a competent engineer who can fulfill all functions
- Typical titles: Engineer, Scientist, Engineering Product Lead, Senior Engineer
- Education: BS + ~4 years

## NASA Insourcing Context

The RFP is a **single-award IDIQ** — meaning one contractor wins the entire contract. This is relevant because:

1. If NASA insources certain roles, they reduce the contract's WYE count (work year equivalents)
2. The RFP includes a phase-in period (Oct 2026 – Jan 2027) for transition
3. The incumbent compensation data package (DRD 1839MA-004) requires Amentum to disclose current rates — meaning NASA already knows what Rick makes

## Contract Structure Details

- **Cost-Plus-Award-Fee (CPAF)** for skills augmentation (majority of work)
- **Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee (CPFF)** option if performance is consistently excellent
- **Firm-Fixed-Price (FFP)** for end-item deliverables
- Award fee pool: up to 100% based on ratings (Excellent=91-100%, Very Good=76-90%, Good=51-75%)
- Fee evaluation: 55% Technical/Management, 25% Cost Control, 15% Schedule, 5% Regulatory
- End-item deliverables capped at 25% of total IDIQ value

### ESSCA II Rate Pressure
- The RFP includes escalation rates for 2027-2035 (typically 3-4%/yr)
- New contractor (or Amentum retaining) must propose rates at or near benchmark
- NASA evaluates "Workforce Compensation, Sustainment and Flexibility" as a proposal subfactor
- Offerors must address "compensation gaps to ensure successful retention of incumbent employees"
- This means the winning contractor is incentivized to raise rates to retain talent
