# SLP Compensation & Career Options Analysis

> Research conducted June 26, 2026 for Rick's wife (CCC-SLP, [[restore-therapy-services]], Madison AL)
> See also: [[wife-slp-profile]], [[restore-therapy-services]]

## A. Is Her Pay and Leave Competitive?

### Pay: No — Significantly Below Market

**Local market data (Huntsville/Madison, AL):**

| Source | Rate |
|---|---|
| Indeed (35 salaries) | avg $42.93/hr for all SLPs |
| Vivian (Madison specifically) | avg $42.39/hr |
| Glassdoor (job postings) | $70K–$105K, most $73K–$99K |
| ZipRecruiter (school SLP) | avg $39.65/hr |

| Comparison | Rate |
|---|---|
| **Your wife (current)** | **$35/hr = ~$70–75K/yr** |
| **Market average** | **$42–43/hr = ~$87–89K/yr** |

She's making roughly **$7–8/hr less** than the average for her area. That's **$14,000–16,000/year** left on the table.

**National benchmarks (CCC-SLP):**

| Benchmark | Source |
|---|---|
| SNF median: $105,500/yr | ASHA 2025 Health Care Survey |
| Hospital median: $100,000–$101,560/yr | ASHA / BLS |
| All healthcare SLPs median: $90,000/yr | ASHA 2025 |
| All SLPs median (all settings): $95,410/yr | BLS May 2024 |
| Home health median: $88,000/yr | ASHA 2025 |
| Outpatient clinic median: $83,000/yr | ASHA 2025 |

She is **$15–35K below national medians** for her credential level.

### The CCC Raise That Didn't Happen

- Reddit r/slp community documents CCC bumps of $3–7/hr (10–20%)
- One SLP: "$5 more/hr for treatment, $7 more/hr for evals"
- Another: "if your supervisor doesn't give you a well-compensated raise then dip"
- Going from $35 → $42/hr would be exactly 20% — bringing her TO market rate, not above
- Restore has a documented company-wide pattern of not giving raises (8+ years per reviews)

### Leave: Also Below Market

- 80 hours/year = 10 days (PTO + sick combined)
- Industry typical: 2–3 weeks (10–15 days) starting PTO, often separate from sick leave
- Restore reviews mention ~4 hrs/biweekly = ~104 hrs/yr for 1–4 years tenure
- Restore forces PTO usage during low census to guarantee 32-hour weeks
- Only 4 paid holidays (many employers offer 6–8+)
- Being "maxed out" on leave = can't take time off AND burning PTO for census gaps

## B. Options to Raise Pay / Get Flexibility

### 1. Negotiate at Restore (Low risk, moderate potential)

- Leverage: CCC + 18 months experience + prior education background + reliable during turnover
- Ask for $42–45/hr citing market rate, national SNF median, CCC credential, reliability
- Risk: Restore has documented history of not giving raises — worth trying once, don't expect much
- Script: "Now that I have my CCCs and 18 months of proven experience here, I'd like to discuss aligning my compensation with the market rate for certified SLPs in our area, which is $42-43/hour. I've been reliable while we've had turnover, and I'd like to stay, but I need to make sure my compensation reflects my credentials and the current market."

### 3. Home Health / PRN Contract Work (Flexible, potentially lucrative)

| Option | Rate |
|---|---|
| PRN in Alabama | $37–$50/hr (ZipRecruiter, Madison) |
| 1099 independent contractor in AL | $49.50–$61/hr (Indeed) |
| National 1099 SLP | $85/treatment, $95/evaluation |
| Home health (ASHA median) | $88,000/yr |

- Could PRN on the side (even 1 day/week) at $45–55/hr to supplement income
- Downside: no benefits, self-employment tax

### 4. Teletherapy / Remote SLP (High flexibility, good pay)

- Remote SLP jobs: $45–$52/hr (VocoVision and others)
- Range: $36–$66/hr depending on state and experience
- 100% work-from-home, flexible scheduling
- School-year teletherapy (2026–2027): full-time, 35 hrs/week
- Her education background = strong asset for school-based teletherapy

**Alabama-specific teletherapy (confirmed viable):**

| Source | Details |
|---|---|
| Indeed | 37 remote SLP openings in Alabama |
| ZipRecruiter | $32–$54/hr for remote SLP in Alabama |
| VocoVision | Actively recruiting AL-licensed SLPs for school-based teletherapy |
| Soliant | Fully remote virtual SLP in Birmingham AL (K–12) |
| ASHA Interstate Compact | Alabama participates — can practice teletherapy across state lines |

She already has everything needed: CCC-SLP, Alabama license, prior education experience.

### 5. School System SLP (Best benefits + schedule, moderate pay)

- Madison County Schools: ~$70,948/yr (Indeed estimate)
- Summers off (2-month break), better PTO, state benefits (retirement, health insurance)
- Pay similar to current, but total package (time off, benefits, schedule) dramatically better
- Prior education experience = exceptionally strong candidate
- May need teaching certification for AL schools (background may fast-track)
- Downside: pay ceiling lower than SNF/hospital

### 6. Hospital SLP (Higher pay + better benefits)

- BLS hospital SLP median: $101,560/yr
- ASHA hospital median: $100,000/yr
- Huntsville Hospital, Crestwood Medical Center are local
- Better PTO, better benefits, higher pay than SNFs
- More structured/less flexible schedule

## Recommendation Summary (Ranked by Ease + Impact)

1. Apply for 1–2 remote SLP teletherapy jobs (VocoVision, Soliant, ProCare) — $45–52/hr, work from home, use offer to negotiate at Restore or leave
2. Apply for local hospital positions (Huntsville Hospital, Crestwood) — $100K+ range, better benefits
3. Present competing offer to Restore — ask for $42–45/hr plus more PTO
4. PRN side work at $45–55/hr to supplement without leaving
5. School system if schedule/benefits matter more than peak pay — summers off, state retirement

Teletherapy route is especially interesting for family situation — remote work, flexible hours, no commute, already well-qualified.
