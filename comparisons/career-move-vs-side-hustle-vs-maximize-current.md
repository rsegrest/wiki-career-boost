# Career Move vs Side-Hustle vs Maximize Current Position

## The Decision

Rick is at a crossroads. 7 years at [[nasa]] via [[amentum]] on the ESSCA contract. $170K salary that hasn't kept pace with inflation. New group is better but it was a lateral move. ESSCA II contract award expected **30 September 2026** — Amentum likely to retain. NASA insourcing initiative is a background risk. The question: stay and maximize, leave for a new role, or build a side income while keeping the day job.

## Path A: Maximize Current Position

### The Play

Stay at Amentum/NASA, ship the proposal/budget planning app, and leverage the ESSCA II re-bid for a rate adjustment. Use the app's "six-figure impact" as a negotiation lever and portfolio centerpiece.

### What You Keep

- $170K salary, $5,100 401k match, 172 hours leave
- ~50% remote work (non-negotiable for family)
- Stability through ESSCA II (Amentum likely retains — Sept 30, 2026 award)
- Current group is professional and accommodating
- Building transferable skills (Electron + React + TypeScript + Dataverse via pacx)

### What You Gain

- **Case study with real ROI**: "Built budget planning app that saved NASA six figures by eliminating spreadsheet errors and duplicate cost entries" — this is gold for future interviews
- **Rate bump**: ESSCA II re-bid may adjust contractor rates closer to inflation. If Amentum wins (likely), push for a raise as part of the new contract
- **Institutional leverage**: 7 years at NASA = deep relationships, domain expertise, credibility
- **Low-stress runway**: New group is calmer — mental bandwidth to build portfolio, post on LinkedIn, or start open-source work on evenings/weekends

### What You Risk

- **NASA insourcing**: General initiative, not targeted at Rick yet — but if it accelerates, contractor positions could face pressure
- **Amentum growth ceiling**: No clear path to principal/staff. You're at the top of the technical track for a contractor on this contract
- **Salary stagnation continues**: If ESSCA II rates don't improve meaningfully, you're stuck at ~$170K with below-inflation raises
- **Opportunity cost**: Every month at $170K with weak raises is a month not at $200K+ elsewhere
- **Skills narrow over time**: Dataverse/PowerApps ecosystem is niche; React+TypeScript skills need active practice outside work

### Key Actions (If You Choose This Path)

1. **Ship the app and document everything** — architecture decisions, tech choices, user impact metrics, before/after cost savings. This is your portfolio's crown jewel.
2. **Push for rate adjustment at ESSCA II award** (Sept 30) — come with inflation data, market comp data, and your app's impact. Ask for $180K–$185K minimum.
3. **Use evenings/weekends for portfolio + LinkedIn** — the calm group gives you mental bandwidth. Post 2–3x/week. Build the [[portfolio-project-ideas|portfolio projects]] from the wiki.
4. **Monitor insourcing closely** — if NASA announces position conversions, be ready to pivot to Path B quickly
5. **Set a deadline**: If no rate improvement by Jan 2027 (3 months post-award), activate Path B

### Best Case Outcome

ESSCA II brings a raise to $185K–$195K. App ships successfully with documented six-figure savings. LinkedIn presence grows. Portfolio has 2–3 shipped projects. You're positioned to either stay comfortable OR leave from strength in 2027.

### Worst Case Outcome

ESSCA II rates stay flat. Insourcing pressure increases. You're still at $170K in 2027 with the same growth ceiling and a narrowing skills window. You've burned another year.

## Path B: Career Move (New Employer)

### The Play

Leave Amentum/NASA for a role at the intersection of React + AI — targeting AI-native startups, remote-first companies, or UX-forward agencies. Use the NASA tenure and the budget app as credibility anchors.

### Target Roles

Based on market research in [[react-ai-job-market-salary-2026]]:

| Role Type | Market Base Salary | Total Comp | Remote | Companies |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Senior Full-Stack (React + AI) | $180K-$220K | $220K-$300K | Remote-first | AI startups, mid-size SaaS |
| AI Engineer (full-stack skills) | $200K-$250K | $250K-$400K | Remote-first | AI-native companies, ML platforms |
| Staff Engineer (React + AI) | $210K-$280K | $280K-$450K | Remote or hybrid | Series B-D startups, mid-size tech |
| Full-Stack Consultant (1099) | $100-$175/hr | — | 100% remote | Multiple clients, flexible |

**"AI Engineer" title carries a 15-25% premium** over "Senior Full-Stack" — negotiate for this title.

**Rick is underpaid by $20K-$50K** at $170K vs market for his skill intersection.

### What You Keep

- Health insurance (wife's plan — portable)
- 401(k) balance (vested, keep it)
- React + TypeScript + AI/agentic skills (market-relevant)
- 7 years NASA pedigree (impressive on resume, especially for defense/space tech)

### What You Gain

- **Salary jump**: $180K–$220K target = $10K–$50K raise, potentially more with equity
- **Growth trajectory**: Real engineering ladder (senior → staff → principal), not contractor ceiling
- **Modern stack**: React production work, not just Dataverse/PowerApps
- **AI immersion**: Work alongside AI engineers, ship LLM features in production
- **Network expansion**: Startup/tech connections vs. gov-contractor bubble
- **Agency over direction**: Choose company, team, tech stack, product mission

### What You Risk

- **Leaving stability**: $170K guaranteed → unknown. First 6 months at new job is always a gamble.
- **Losing 401(k) match**: $5,100/yr gone (but salary increase should more than cover)
- **Remote uncertainty**: Must negotiate hard for remote-first. Some "remote" jobs drift hybrid.
- **Resume gap narrative**: If it doesn't work out, explaining "left NASA contractor for startup that didn't pan out" is harder than "left NASA for a better opportunity"
- **Timing risk**: ESSCA II award is Sept 30 — if you leave before the award, you forgo any rate adjustment. If you leave after and Amentum loses, you might have been displaced anyway.
- **Current app unfinished**: If you leave before the budget app ships, you lose the case study and the "six-figure impact" metric

### Known Gaps to Address Before Interviews

From [[rick-segrest-pro|profile]]:
- **System design at scale** — haven't built for >10k concurrent users. Study: designing data-intensive applications, distributed systems primer.
- **AI production experience** — intense learning but limited deployed LLM features. Ship a demo first.
- **Team leadership** — IC track, limited formal management. Position as "tech lead" not "manager" if aiming staff.
- **Mobile** — React Native minimal. Don't apply for mobile-heavy roles.

### Key Actions (If You Choose This Path)

1. **Ship the budget app first** (or at least get it to a demoable state) — the case study is your strongest interview asset
2. **Update resume** — achievement-focused, 2 pages. Lead with: "7 years NASA, built budget app saving six figures, React+TypeScript+AI"
3. **Rebuild portfolio site** — Next.js, case studies, the budget app, the [[portfolio-project-ideas|portfolio projects]]
4. **Start applying Sept 1** — 30 days before ESSCA II award gives you optionality
5. **Target 5–10 applications/week** to remote-first AI startups and devtools companies
6. **LinkedIn ramp** — 3 posts/week for 60 days before applying. Build the "AI-powered UI" narrative.
7. **Practice system design interviews** — 2 hours/week for 8 weeks covers the gap

### Best Case Outcome

Land a remote-first Senior/Staff Engineer role at $200K+ with equity, modern stack, and real growth trajectory. The NASA pedigree + budget app case study close the deal. 2027 is a breakout year.

### Worst Case Outcome

Leave in September, ESSCA II brings a raise you missed. New job is a culture fit disaster or the startup runs out of runway in 6 months. You're job-hunting again with a shorter tenure at the new place and a gap at NASA.

### Mitigation

Don't resign until you have a signed offer. Let the ESSCA II award happen — if Amentum wins and offers a raise, you have leverage at both the new employer and Amentum. If Amentum loses, you already have the exit planned.

## Path C: Side-Hustle (Freelance/Consulting While Employed)

### The Play

Keep the $170K day job. Use evenings, weekends, and the ~50% remote days to build a freelance/consulting practice in React, AI integration, or agent development. Grow it until it either replaces the day job income or becomes a permanent second revenue stream.

### What You Keep

- Everything from Path A: $170K, 401k match, leave, stability, health insurance
- Low burnout risk from day job (new group is calm)
- ESSCA II rate bump potential (if it comes)

### What You Gain

- **Additional income**: $20K–$80K/yr depending on hours and rate
  - At $100/hr × 10 hrs/week × 48 weeks = $48,000
  - At $150/hr × 10 hrs/week × 48 weeks = $72,000
  - At $150/hr × 15 hrs/week × 48 weeks = $108,000
- **Portfolio building**: Client work = case studies. Each project is a LinkedIn post and portfolio piece.
- **Market intelligence**: Pricing your time teaches you what skills are worth. Direct feedback from paying clients is more honest than salary surveys.
- **Network expansion**: Clients become references, referrals, and full-time offers
- **Optionality**: If the side income hits $80K+, you can decide whether to go full-time independent — from a position of strength, not desperation
- **Low risk**: If it doesn't work, you still have $170K and a day job

### What You Risk

- **Burnout**: Two jobs is real. 10–15 hrs/week on top of a full-time job is sustainable for ~6 months, then it starts to hurt. Family pressures make this especially dangerous.
- **Conflict of interest**: Amentum/NASA contract may have moonlighting clauses or IP restrictions. Must check before accepting paid side work.
- **Quality dilution**: Spreading thin means neither the day job nor side work gets your best
- **Slow ramp**: First 3–6 months are client acquisition, not billable. You'll work unpaid hours building the pipeline.
- **No benefits on side income**: 1099 income = self-employment tax (15.3%), no 401k match, no PTO. Effective rate is lower than it looks.
- **Time scarcity**: With family pressures and ~50% remote, evenings/weekends are the only windows. That's 8–15 hrs/week realistically.

### Realistic Income Calculation

Based on freelance market data in [[react-ai-job-market-salary-2026]]:

| Scenario | Rate | Hours/Week | Weeks/Year | Gross | SE Tax (15.3%) | Net |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | $120/hr | 8 | 44 | $42,240 | $6,463 | $35,777 |
| Moderate | $150/hr | 10 | 48 | $72,000 | $11,016 | $60,984 |
| Aggressive | $175/hr | 12 | 48 | $100,800 | $15,422 | $85,378 |

Rick's specific skills (React + TypeScript + LangChain + RAG + MCP + agent orchestration) support $120-$175/hr. Specialized agent orchestration or custom RAG pipeline work can reach $150-$225/hr.

### Key Actions (If You Choose This Path)

1. **Check Amentum's moonlighting policy FIRST** — read your employment agreement. If there's a non-compete or moonlighting clause, you need written permission or a side gig that doesn't conflict.
2. **Define your offering** — pick ONE thing: "React + AI integration for small businesses" or "Agent/LLM consulting for startups." Don't be a generalist.
3. **Set up the infrastructure** — LLC (or sole prop to start), business bank account, invoice template, simple contract template (use Stripe Atlas or similar)
4. **Get first client in 30 days** — tap your LinkedIn network (377 connections), post about what you're offering, or use Upwork/Contra for initial traction
5. **Cap at 12 hrs/week** — anything more risks the day job and family. If demand exceeds capacity, raise rates.
6. **Track everything** — hours, rates, client acquisition time, expenses. This data tells you when (or if) to go full-time independent.
7. **Reassess at 6 months** — if side income is >$30K net and growing, consider reducing day job to 32 hrs/week (if Amentum allows) or transitioning to full-time freelance

### Best Case Outcome

Side income hits $50K–$80K net by month 12. You have a case study portfolio, a client base, and the option to go independent at $200K+ total comp. ESSCA II rate bump adds another $10K–$15K to the day job. Total income: $220K–$265K with full optionality.

### Worst Case Outcome

Burnout by month 4. Side income is $5K–$10K (mostly unpaid client acquisition time). Family pressures suffer. Day job quality drops. You shut it down and go back to Path A with less energy than when you started.

### Mitigation

Start SMALL. One client, 5 hrs/week. If that's sustainable for 60 days, scale to 10. The biggest mistake is overcommitting before you know the rhythm works.

## The ESSCA II Variable

**Award date: 30 September 2026**

RFP data extracted and analyzed in [[essca-ii-rfp-analysis]]. Key facts:

- **Contract value:** $3.248B max, 8.5-year IDIQ (single award)
- **Phase-in:** Oct 2026 – Jan 2027 (rate adjustments likely here)
- **Base period:** Feb 2027 – Jan 2032 (5 years)
- **Escalation:** RFP includes annual escalation rates for CY2027-2035 (typically 3-4%/yr)
- **ERI benchmark rates:** Government-published market rates for each labor category
- **Rick's positioning:** $81.42/hr vs ES-8 benchmark $84.46/hr = ~$6K below market
- **Retention incentive:** RFP requires offerors to address "compensation gaps to ensure successful retention of incumbent employees"
- **Workforce compensation** is a evaluated proposal subfactor — NASA scores it

### Rick's Rate vs. ERI Benchmarks (MSFC, unburdened hourly)

| Level | Benchmark | Annualized | vs. Rick ($81.42/hr) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ES-9 | $85.29/hr | $177,885 | +$7,885 above Rick |
| ES-8 | $84.46/hr | $176,165 | +$6,165 above Rick |
| ES-7 | $78.38/hr | $163,510 | -$6,490 below Rick |
| ES-6 | $73.13/hr | $152,568 | -$17,432 below Rick |

Rick is paid between ES-7 and ES-8 benchmarks. If he's classified as ES-8, he's underpaid by ~$6K. The RFP's compensation gap requirement means the winning contractor is incentivized to close that gap.

This is the pivotal near-term event. Three scenarios:

### Scenario 1: Amentum retains ESSCA II, rates improve
- Path A becomes much more attractive — push for $185K+ (ES-8 benchmark + escalation)
- The RFP's compensation gap requirement gives Rick leverage: "I'm $6K below ERI benchmark for my level"
- Phase-in period (Oct-Jan) is when rate adjustments happen
- Path B becomes less urgent but still valid if ceiling is capped
- Path C is the safest play — keep the raised day job + side income

### Scenario 2: Amentum retains ESSCA II, rates flat
- Path A loses appeal — you're stuck at $170K, still below benchmark
- The RFP requires escalation rates, so "flat" is unlikely — but Amentum could pocket the difference
- Path B becomes the priority — the app case study is your ticket out
- Path C is viable but you're side-hustling on top of a below-market salary

### Scenario 3: Amentum loses ESSCA II
- Path A is destabilized — new contractor may or may not keep you
- New contractor is incentivized to retain incumbents (RFP requirement) — could be a raise opportunity
- Path B becomes essential — you need a landing spot before the transition
- Path C is a bridge — side income cushions the transition gap

**Rick's assessment:** Amentum likely retains the contract. This favors Path A (near-term) or Path C (long-term), with Path B as a parallel preparation track.

## Recommended Strategy: A+C Hybrid with B Preparation

The optimal play given your constraints:

**Phase 1: Now through Sept 30, 2026 (3 months)**
- Stay at Amentum. Ship the budget app. Document the case study.
- Update resume, rebuild portfolio site, ramp LinkedIn to 3 posts/week.
- Check Amentum moonlighting policy.
- Start ONE small side project (5 hrs/week max) to test the freelance waters.
- Begin casual job browsing — don't apply yet, but identify 10–20 target companies.

**Phase 2: Sept 30 – Dec 31, 2026 (ESSCA II award + 3 months)**
- If Amentum retains and rates improve: negotiate hard for $185K+. Continue Path A+C.
- If Amentum retains but rates flat: activate Path B applications. Use the app case study.
- If Amentum loses: Path B is now emergency mode. You have the case study and resume ready.
- Side-hustle: if first client went well, scale to 8–10 hrs/week. If not, reassess.

**Phase 3: Jan–June 2027**
- If still at Amentum and happy: maximize Path A + C. Side income should be $20K+ by now.
- If in new job: focus on ramp-up, don't side-hustle during first 6 months.
- If full-time freelance: side income has replaced day job. Make the jump.

This strategy preserves optionality at every branch. You never foreclose a path until the data tells you to.

### How to Find Out About ESSCA II Rate Adjustments

1. **Ask your Amentum program manager or contract lead** — they should know the bid terms. Frame it as: "I'm hearing the new contract might include rate adjustments — can you share what that looks like for my position?"
2. **Check the SAM.gov solicitation** — the ESSCA II RFP would be public. Search "ESSCA" at sam.gov. The RFP typically includes a "wage determination" or "rate schedule" section.
3. **Ask Amentum HR during the re-bid transition** — they'll be communicating changes to employees as part of the new contract. Watch for all-hands meetings or email blasts in September.
4. **Talk to colleagues who've been through re-bids before** — 7-year veterans at NASA have seen contract transitions. Ask what happened to rates last time.
