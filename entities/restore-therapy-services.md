## Compensation Patterns

- **No raises for extended periods** — multiple reviewers cite no raises for 8+ years
- **Maximum wage caps** per discipline that reportedly haven't changed
- **Low starting pay** for new graduates
- **No incentives** for meeting productivity goals
- One review: "The company has not given raises in over 8 years along with they have a maximum wage for each discipline"
- Another: "Long time therapists are pushed out for low wage grads"
- Company HR defends pay as "competitive" citing total compensation (PTO, 4 holidays, insurance, 401k match)

## PTO & Scheduling

- ~4 hours PTO per biweekly pay period (1–4 years tenure) = ~104 hrs/yr
- PTO usage forced during low census to guarantee 32-hour weeks
- PTO payout denied upon departure (one SLP reported not being paid out after 30-day notice)
- Cannot take PTO without coverage — sole-discipline therapists effectively cannot take time off
- Only 4 paid holidays for clinical staff
- Schedule confirmation often only 1–2 weeks in advance
- Required to work one weekend per month (6-day work weeks)
- Full-time therapists not guaranteed full-time hours
- May be required to travel between facilities to get hours

## Productivity Requirements

- 88% productivity required for assistants (as of 2023)
- High caseloads with fluctuating volume
- Group and concurrent therapy increasingly enforced

## Culture

- Coworkers consistently praised across reviews
- Favoritism reported — advancement tied to seniority or personal relationships
- Company described as focused on "the bottom line"
- Sign-on bonuses come with 2-year contract — must repay if you leave early
